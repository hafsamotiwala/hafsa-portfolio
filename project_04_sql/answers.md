### Q1: Top 10 Products by Total Sales

**Insight:**
The highest revenue-generating products are expensive office equipment such as copiers,
binding machines, and conferencing systems. This indicates a B2B-focused sales model where
revenue is driven by low-volume but high-value items rather than frequent low-cost purchases.

### Q2: Top 10 Products by Quantity Sold

**Insight:**
High sales volume is driven by low-cost, frequently purchased office supplies.
This contrasts with revenue-based rankings (Query 1), which are dominated by expensive equipment.
Together, these analyses highlight a dual sales strategy:
- High-volume consumables for everyday demand
- High-value equipment for large, infrequent purchases
This insight can guide inventory planning and promotional focus.

### Q3: Sales by Region & Product Category

**Insight:**
Revenue distribution varies across regions. For instance, in the East region, Technology dominates,
while Furniture and Office Supplies also contribute significantly. In the West, Technology and Furniture
perform strongly, with Office Supplies slightly lower. Understanding these patterns helps with
targeted marketing, regional inventory planning, and identifying high-demand product categories
per region.

### Q4: Monthly Sales Trend

**Insight:**
Monthly sales data from 2014 to 2017 shows clear seasonal patterns and gradual growth over the years.
Some months, like November and December, consistently show higher sales, indicating peak demand periods.
Lower-sales months, such as February, suggest off-season periods. Understanding these trends can help
with inventory planning, marketing campaigns, and staffing, ensuring resources are allocated efficiently
during high-demand months.

### Q5: Total Profit by Customer Segment

**Insight:**
The Consumer segment generates the highest total profit, followed by Corporate and Home Office.
This indicates that individual consumers are the most valuable customers in terms of profitability.
Targeting marketing efforts and promotions toward the Consumer segment could further increase revenue,
while maintaining relationships with Corporate and Home Office segments ensures balanced growth.

**Insight:**
Standard Class generates the highest total sales, followed by Second Class, First Class, and Same Day.

### Q9: Rolling 3-Month Revenue

Insight:
The rolling 3-month revenue highlights short-term trends and seasonality in sales.  
By summing the current month with the previous two months, fluctuations are smoothed, making 
patterns like peak months (November-December) and slower months (February) more visible.  
This is useful for inventory planning, marketing campaigns, and cash flow management.
